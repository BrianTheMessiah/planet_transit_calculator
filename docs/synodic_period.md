# Synodic Period/Orbital Period 
- Orbital Period: Amount of time for a celestial object to complete one orbit around another object 
    - Orbital period is determined by a 360 degree revolution of one body around its primary 
    - Examples
        - The Earth orbiting the Sun 
        - A satellite orbiting Earth 
### Small Body Orbiting a Central Body 
- Keplers Third Law
    - $$ T = 2\pi * \sqrt{\frac{a^3}{GM}} $$
    - $a$: Orbits semi-major axis 
    - $G$: Gravitational constant 
        - **What is the gravitation constant?**
            - Empirical physical constant giving the strenght of the gravitational field induced by mass 
    - $M$: Mass of the more massive body
